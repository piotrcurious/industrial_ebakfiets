# Brakes and Steering Module

The brakes and steering of the industrial bakfiets are designed to handle high loads and provide safe, predictable control.

## Steering System
### 1. Steering Geometry
- **Design**: Low-slung steering linkage with heavy-duty rod ends.
- **Steering Linkage Rod**: **15mm OD x 2.0mm WT** steel tubing.
- **Rod Ends**: Standard industrial M10 or M12 rod ends.
- **Thread Engagement**: Minimum **20mm** engagement on all rod end threads to prevent pull-out under load.

### 2. Steering Stops
To prevent the steering linkage from binding or the front wheel from hitting the cargo box, physical stops are integrated:
- **Design**: 10mm solid steel pins welded to the fork crown.
- **Interface**: Matching 6mm reinforced steel strike plates welded to the head tube housing.
- **Travel Limit**: Exactly **+/- 45 degrees** from center.

### 3. Dimensions, Clearances & Fits
- **Steering Column OD**: 15.0mm precision steel rod.
- **Bearing**: Pair of 7202 Angular Contact Bearings.

## Braking System
The industrial bakfiets uses a triple-redundant braking system.

### 1. Front Braking (Main Stopping Power)
- **Disc Rotor**: 203mm diameter, 2.0mm thick (6-bolt pattern).
- **Caliper**: Hydraulic 4-piston caliper (PM mount).

### 2. Rear Braking (Control and Parking)
- **Disc Rotor**: 203mm diameter.
- **Caliper**: Hydraulic/Mechanical (IS mount).
- **Parking Brake**: A **locking brake lever** is required on the rear brake.

## Component Sourcing
In budget builds, salvaged **Calipers/Rotors** from mountain bikes or small mopeds can be used, provided they match the 203mm/180mm sizing and mounting standards (PM/IS).

## Tolerances and Fit
- **Steering Alignment**: Within +/- 1 degree.
- **Stop-Pin Tolerance**: +/- 0.5mm to ensure symmetrical turning radius.
