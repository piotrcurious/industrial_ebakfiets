# Brakes and Steering Module

The brakes and steering of the industrial bakfiets are designed to handle high loads and provide safe, predictable control.

## Steering System
### 1. Steering Geometry
- **Ackermann Steering**: If the design is a trike (not specified in the original README but often implied for heavy cargo), Ackermann steering would be necessary.
- **Single-Pivot (Bakfiets style)**: For a two-wheeled cargo bike, a low-slung steering linkage with heavy-duty rod ends.

### 2. Steering Linkage
- **Rod Ends**: Standard industrial rod ends (e.g., M10 or M12) for durability and easy adjustment.
- **Linkage Rod**: 12mm-15mm diameter steel tubing or 10mm-12mm solid steel rod.

### 3. Headset and Bearings
- **Type**: Standard 1-1/8" or 1.5" bicycle headsets or industrial tapered roller bearings for extreme loads.

## Braking System
### 1. Front Braking
- **Regenerative Braking**: The front 250W direct drive hub motor provides strong, adjustable electric braking.
- **Mechanical Backup**: A mechanical or hydraulic disc brake with 180mm-203mm rotors for maximum stopping power.

### 2. Rear Braking
- **Disc or Drum**: Standard 160mm-180mm disc brake or internal drum brake integrated into the rear hub motor.

### 3. Brake Levers
- **E-Brake Levers**: High-quality alloy levers with integrated electrical cut-off switches to stop the motors when braking.
- **Parking Brake**: A locking brake lever is essential for a heavy cargo bike when loading/unloading on an incline.

## Safety and Control
- **Handlebars**: Wide, ergonomic handlebars for better leverage when the cargo box is fully loaded.
- **Steering Damper**: Optional but recommended to prevent steering wobble at higher speeds or under load.
