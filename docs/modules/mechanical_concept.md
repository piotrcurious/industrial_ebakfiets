# Industrial E-Bakfiets: Mechanical Concept

This module describes the core mechanical architecture of the heavy-duty cargo bike, redesigned using a **Surface-to-Surface Connection** logic starting from the front automotive tire.

## 1. Assembly Chain
The design follows a logical physical connection path:
1.  **Front Tire (Anchor)**: 155/70 R13 automotive tire.
2.  **Front Hub Motor**: Mated to the tire rim center.
3.  **Front Fork**: Mated to the hub's M14 axle ends.
4.  **Steering Head**: Mated to the fork's 15mm steering shaft via 7202 bearings.
5.  **Main Frame**: Mated to the steering head's structural gusset.
6.  **Rear End**: Mated to the frame spars, including the 16" rear wheel and BB shell.

## 2. Key Dimensions
- **Wheelbase**: 1950 mm
- **Head Angle**: 68°
- **Trail**: ~73 mm (calculated from 45mm rake and 548mm tire OD)
- **Bed Width**: 810 mm (Standard Euro-pallet compatibility)
- **Ground Clearance**: 150 mm

## 3. Structural Elements
- **Frame Spars**: 40x40x2.5mm rectangular steel tubing.
- **Steering Head**: 1-1/4" Schedule 40 steel pipe (35.05mm ID).
- **Fork Blades**: 30x30x2.5mm rectangular steel tubing with 6mm laser-cut dropouts.

## 4. Design Philosophy
By starting the design from the "extreme" part (the front tire), we ensure that the most geometrically constrained component (the large automotive wheel) dictates the clearances for the fork and frame, rather than trying to "fit" the wheel into a pre-existing frame design.
