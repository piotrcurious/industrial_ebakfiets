# Testing and Validation Module

To ensure the Industrial E-Bakfiets meets its heavy-duty performance targets safely, the following testing protocols must be followed after assembly and before commissioning for cargo use.

## 1. Static Load Testing
Verify the structural integrity of the frame and cargo bed.
- **Protocol**: Gradually load the cargo bed with sandbags or weighted plates up to **350kg**.
- **Observation**: Check for visible deflection in the 40x40mm main spars. Deflection at the center of the bed should not exceed **5mm**.
- **Weld Inspection**: Inspect all critical weldments (Header plate, Gooseneck, Dropouts) for hairline cracks or paint flaking using a magnifying glass.

## 2. Dynamic Braking Test
Verify the stopping power of the 203mm/180mm hydraulic brake system.
- **Protocol**: With a **100kg** test load, accelerate to **20km/h** and perform an emergency stop.
- **Requirement**: The vehicle must come to a complete stop within **4.5 meters** on dry pavement.
- **Brake Fade**: Repeat 5 times in rapid succession. Check for lever "sponginess" or significant reduction in stopping power.

## 3. Steering and Linkage Validation
Ensure the dual bell crank system is reliable and binding-free.
- **Protocol**: Perform a "Full-Lock-to-Lock" test while stationary with a 100kg load in the box.
- **Observation**: Listen for clicking or grinding in the 7202 bearings. Verify that the steering tie-rod does not contact the frame or cargo box at any point in the 90-degree total sweep.
- **Centering**: Ride in a straight line at 10km/h. Release the handlebars momentarily (if safe) to verify the vehicle does not pull to one side.

## 4. Electrical Stress Test
Verify the thermal management and power delivery of the dual-motor system.
- **Protocol**: Navigate a **10% grade** hill for at least 500 meters with a 150kg total load.
- **Monitoring**: Use the Supervisory Controller to monitor motor temperatures.
- **Threshold**: Neither motor should exceed **75°C** during this climb.
- **Redundancy Test**: Temporarily disconnect the front motor. Verify the rear geared motor can initiate a hill-start under load without stalling or excessive overheating.

## 5. Vibration and Hardware Audit
- **Protocol**: Ride over a 50-meter section of "cobblestone" or rough gravel.
- **Inspection**: Immediately re-torque the **M8x140mm Rim Bolts** and **M14 Axle Nuts**. If any have loosened, apply high-strength thread locker and re-test.
