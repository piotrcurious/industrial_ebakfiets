# Mechanical Geometry Module

The handling and stability of the industrial bakfiets depend on precise frame and fork geometry, optimized for heavy cargo and low speeds.

## Frame Geometry
### 1. Head Angle
- **Specification**: **68 degrees**.
- **Reasoning**: A slacker head angle provides increased stability and better tracking when the front cargo box is fully loaded.

### 2. Steering Axis Offset (Rake)
- **Specification**: **35mm rake**.
- **Resulting Trail**: **~73mm** (with 548mm diameter 13\" tire).
- **Behavior**: This trail measurement offers a balance between low-speed maneuverability and high-speed stability, reducing the "flopping" feel common in cargo bikes.

## Front Fork Design
The custom-welded fork must handle extreme lateral and braking loads from the automotive tire and 203mm rotor.

### 1. Dimensions
- **Crown Inner Width**: 200mm (Provides 22.5mm clearance on each side of the 155mm tire).
- **Axle-to-Crown Length**: 350mm.
- **Steerer Tube Length**: 300mm (15mm precision rod).

### 2. Construction
- **Legs**: 40x20x3mm rectangular steel tubing or 32mm x 3mm round pipe.
- **Crown**: 10mm thick steel plate, laser-cut to include integrated mounting tabs for the BLDC housing box.
- **Dropouts**: 8mm thick reinforced steel plates with 10.2mm slots and integrated torque arm bolt holes.

## Rear Frame Geometry
- **Wheelbase**: 1850mm - 2000mm (Depending on cargo box length).
- **Chainstay Length**: 450mm.
- **Bottom Bracket Drop**: 50mm (Ensures stability by lowering the center of gravity).

## Mechanical Clearances
- **Tire to Frame**: Minimum 20mm at all points.
- **Ground Clearance**: 150mm (At the lowest point of the frame rails).
