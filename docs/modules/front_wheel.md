# Front Wheel Module

The front wheel of the industrial bakfiets is designed for heavy-duty cargo transport, utilizing standard automotive parts for durability and ease of maintenance.

## Components
### 1. 13-inch Car Tire (155/70 R13)
- **Standardization**: This is a very common, inexpensive car tire size.
- **Dimensions**:
  - **Width**: 155mm (Actual may vary from 152mm to 158mm).
  - **Diameter**: 548mm nominal.
- **Load Capacity**: Rated for approximately 350kg, far exceeding standard bicycle tires.
- **Durability**: High puncture resistance and long service life.

### 2. Two-Part "Split" Rim
- **Design**: Similar to wheelbarrow or Piaggio Ape rims. It consists of two halves bolted together.
- **Dimensions**:
  - **Diameter**: 13-inch.
  - **Bolt Pattern**: Standard 4x98mm or 4x100mm.
- **Maintenance**: Allows for easy tire and tube replacement without the need for specialized tire shop equipment.

### 3. Inner Tube
- **Specification**: 13-inch automotive tube (G13/G14 size) with Schrader valve.

### 4. Front Hub Motor
- **Type**: 250W Direct Drive.
- **Axle Dimensions & Fits**:
  - **Main Shaft Diameter**: 14mm.
  - **Flats Diameter**: 10.0mm.
  - **Dropout Slot**: 10.2mm (**Loose-Fit, 0.2mm clearance**).
  - **Dropout Width**: 175mm.
- **Bearings**: **Internal 6202-series sealed bearings** (standard for most hub motors).

## Mechanical Integration & Assembly
### 1. Axle to Dropout Fit
- **Locking Method**: The axle is fixed using **M14 flange nuts** and high-torque washers (torque arms).
- **Torque Arm**: Essential for direct-drive motors. A 4mm-6mm steel plate torque arm is bolted to the fork leg to prevent the axle from spinning and damaging the dropouts.

### 2. Rim to Motor Integration
- **Connection**: Custom laser-cut 5mm steel flange plates.
- **Locking**: 4 to 6 **M8 Grade 10.9 bolts** with nyloc nuts secure the split rim halves and the motor flange.

## Assembly & Disassembly Methods
### Assembly
1. Place the inner tube inside the tire.
2. Sandwich the tire between the two halves of the split rim.
3. Position the motor and flange plates in the center.
4. Pass M8 bolts through the assembly and tighten to **25-30Nm**.
5. Slide the motor axle into the fork dropouts.
6. Attach torque arms and tighten M14 nuts to **40-50Nm**.

### Disassembly
1. Deflate the tire fully.
2. Loosen M14 axle nuts and remove the wheel from the fork.
3. Unbolt the M8 rim bolts.
4. Separate the rim halves to access the tube and tire.

## Tolerances
- **Axle-to-Dropout**: 0.2mm clearance (Loose-fit).
- **Rim Parallelism**: Within +/- 0.5mm across the rim diameter.
