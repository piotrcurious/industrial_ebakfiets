# Manufacturing Jigs and Alignment Module

Building a precise industrial frame requires specialized jigs to ensure all components are aligned within engineering tolerances.

## Frame Jig Design
### 1. Main Base
- **Material**: Flat steel plate (10mm thick) or a heavy-duty level wooden workbench.
- **Size**: At least 1000mm x 2500mm.

### 2. Spar Alignment Guides
- **Components**: **50x50x5mm Steel Angle Iron** bolted to the base plate.
- **Function**: These guides lock the two main 40x40mm spars in a perfectly parallel position. The outer width of the spars is set to 834mm to match the cargo box exterior.

### 3. Head Tube V-Block
- **Material**: Machined steel or hardwood V-block.
- **Function**: Centers the 1-1/4\" Sch 40 head tube pipe relative to the frame centerline and maintains the specified head angle (68 degrees).

## Alignment Techniques
### 1. Dropout Squareness
- **Method**: Use a **135mm precision spacer** (dummy axle) clamped in the rear dropouts during welding to prevent warping and ensure the axle sits perpendicular to the frame spars.

### 2. Centering the Steering Column
- **Method**: Use a plumb line or laser level from the head tube center to the midpoint of the rear dropout spacer.
- **Tolerance**: The front and rear wheel tracks must be aligned within **+/- 2mm**.

### 3. Cargo Bed Flatness
- **Method**: Check diagonal measurements corner-to-corner on the 1350mm x 810mm bed spars.
- **Tolerance**: Corner-to-corner difference should not exceed **3mm**.

## Assembly Tips
- **Tack Welding**: Always tack weld the entire frame while in the jig before performing final structural welds.
- **Heat Management**: Weld in short sections to prevent localized heat distortion of the long frame rails.
- **Post-Weld Inspection**: After cooling, verify that the 7202 bearings slip into the head tube as designed.
