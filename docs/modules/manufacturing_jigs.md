# Manufacturing Jigs and Alignment Module

Building a precise industrial frame requires specialized jigs to ensure all components are aligned within engineering tolerances.

## Frame Jig Design
### 1. Main Base (The "Reference Table")
- **Material**: Flat steel plate (10mm thick) or a heavy-duty level wooden workbench reinforced with steel channels.
- **Size**: At least 1000mm x 2500mm.
- **Grid**: Mark a centerline and 100mm grid lines across the entire surface using a scribe or permanent marker.

### 2. Spar Alignment Guides
- **Components**: **50x50x5mm Steel Angle Iron** sections (approx. 200mm long) bolted or clamped to the base plate.
- **Function**: These guides lock the two main 40x40mm spars in a perfectly parallel position.
- **Setup**: Position the inner faces of the guides at exactly **834mm** apart to match the required cargo bed exterior width.

### 3. Head Tube V-Block & Riser Jig
- **Material**: Machined steel V-block or a "V" notch cut into 40x40mm tubing.
- **Angle Support**: A vertical 40x40mm pillar welded to the base plate at the front. Use an adjustable brace to lock the V-block at **68 degrees** relative to the base table.
- **Elevation**: The center of the V-block must be held **650mm** above the base table to accommodate the "Gooseneck" riser geometry.

### 4. Steering Hub Dummy Axle
- **Tool**: A 15mm precision rod (stepped to 12mm or 10mm if needed).
- **Function**: Insert through the head tube during welding to ensure the tube doesn't warp and that the steering axis remains perfectly collinear.

## Alignment Techniques
### 1. Dropout Squareness
- **Method**: Use a **135mm precision spacer** (dummy axle) clamped in the rear dropouts during welding to prevent warping and ensure the axle sits perpendicular to the frame spars.

### 2. Centering the Steering Column
- **Method**: Use a plumb line or laser level from the head tube center to the midpoint of the rear dropout spacer.
- **Tolerance**: The front and rear wheel tracks must be aligned within **+/- 2mm**.

### 3. Cargo Bed Flatness
- **Method**: Check diagonal measurements corner-to-corner on the 1350mm x 810mm bed spars.
- **Tolerance**: Corner-to-corner difference should not exceed **3mm**.

## Assembly Tips
- **Tack Welding**: Always tack weld the entire frame (at least 4 points per joint) while in the jig before performing final structural welds.
- **Heat Management**: Weld in short (20mm) sections, alternating between left and right sides of the frame to prevent the 1950mm rails from "bowing" during cooling.
- **Squareness Check**: Before final welding, measure the diagonals of the cargo bed ($A-C$ and $B-D$). They must be within **2mm** of each other.
- **Post-Weld Inspection**: After cooling, verify that the 7202 bearings slip into the head tube. If the tube has shrunk slightly, use a 35mm adjustable reamer to restore the bearing seat.
