# Regulatory Compliance and Safety Standards

The Industrial E-Bakfiets is designed as a heavy-duty Electrically Power Assisted Cycle (EPAC). Depending on the jurisdiction, different regulations apply.

## 1. European Union (EN 15194)
To comply with the standard EPAC regulations in the EU:
- **Continuous Power**: The sum of both motors should be electronically limited to **250W continuous**.
- **Cut-off Speed**: Assistance must cut off at **25km/h**.
- **Activation**: Assistance must only be provided when the rider is pedaling (PAS sensor mandatory). Throttles are permitted only for "walk-assist" up to 6km/h.

*Note: In many jurisdictions, heavy cargo bikes can be registered as 'L-category' vehicles if they exceed these limits, requiring insurance and a license.*

## 2. North America (Class 1, 2, and 3)
The dual-motor system can be configured to meet US e-bike classes:
- **Class 1**: Pedal-assist only, 20mph limit.
- **Class 2**: Throttle-assist permitted, 20mph limit.
- **Class 3**: Pedal-assist only, 28mph limit.
- **Power Limit**: Typically 750W (1.0 hp).

## 3. Structural Safety Standards
The design incorporates safety factors derived from industrial equipment standards:
- **Fastener Safety**: Critical joints (axles, steering, brakes) use Grade 10.9 or 12.9 fasteners to provide a 4x safety factor against shear failure under maximum load (350kg).
- **Braking Performance**: The 203mm front rotor is sized to dissipate the heat generated by stopping a 450kg total system weight (vehicle + rider + cargo) from 25km/h on a 10% grade.
- **Redundancy**: The dual-motor system provides "limp-home" capability; if one motor or controller fails, the second can provide enough torque to move the vehicle to a safe service location.

## 4. Electrical Safety
- **Fuse Protection**: Each battery circuit must include a high-current DC fuse (e.g., 40A MIDI fuse).
- **IP Rating**: All enclosures and connectors are specified as **IP65** or higher to protect against water ingress during rain or industrial cleaning.
