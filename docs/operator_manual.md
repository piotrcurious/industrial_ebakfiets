# Operator's Manual: Industrial E-Bakfiets

Welcome to your heavy-duty cargo vehicle. This manual covers the basic operation, safety limits, and best practices for the Industrial E-Bakfiets.

## 1. Safety Information
- **Maximum Payload**: 350kg (Cargo only).
- **Maximum Speed**: 25km/h (Assisted).
- **Braking**: Always use both front and rear brakes. The front brake provides ~70% of stopping power.
- **Center of Gravity**: Place heavy items at the bottom of the cargo box, centered between the side spars.

## 2. Startup Procedure
1. **Check Tires**: Ensure front tire is firm (45 psi).
2. **Power On**: Turn on both battery packs (or the master switch).
3. **Monitor Check**: Observe the Supervisory Controller display. Verify battery voltage is >42.0V.
4. **Throttle Test**: Gently blip the throttle to confirm both motors are responding.

## 3. Riding with Cargo
- **Starting**: Use the rear geared motor (pedal assist or gentle throttle) to get moving. The front motor is most efficient once you are at a walking pace.
- **Cornering**: Slow down before turns. The long 1950mm wheelbase requires wider turning circles than a standard bicycle.
- **Climbing**: For steep hills, maintain a steady cadence and allow the geared rear motor to provide the bulk of the torque.

## 4. Charging
- Use the provided dual-output or two separate chargers.
- Charge in a well-ventilated area.
- Do not leave the batteries charging unattended for more than 12 hours.

## 5. Emergency Procedures
- **Electrical Cutoff**: If the throttle sticks, pull either brake lever; the built-in cutoff sensors will kill power to both motors.
- **Overheat Alarm**: If the Supervisory Controller buzzes, slow down or stop and allow the motors to cool. This typically occurs only during prolonged heavy climbing in hot weather.
